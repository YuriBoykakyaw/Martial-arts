Martials Art

Martial arts are codified systems and traditions of combat practiced for a number of reasons such as self-defense; military and law enforcement applications!

Karate

  • First, karate can help improve your physical fitness. If you’re looking to get in shape or lose weight, karate is a great way to do it. You’ll burn calories and build muscle while learning self-defence techniques.
  • Second, karate can help improve your mental well-being. Studies have shown that regular exercise can help reduce stress and anxiety levels. Karate is an excellent form of exercise for relieving stress and promoting relaxation.
  • Finally, karate can help you develop discipline and focus. As an adult, you likely have many demands on your time and attention.

    • Judo

    While judo includes a variety of rolls, falls, throws, hold downs, chokes, joint-locks, and strikes, the primary focus is on throwing (nage-waza), and groundwork (ne-waza). Throws are divided in two groups of techniques, standing techniques (tachi-waza), and sacrifice techniques (sutemi-waza).

    Sumo

    Sumo (相撲, sumō) is a Japanese-style of wrestling and Japan's national sport. It originated in ancient times as a performance to entertain the Shinto deities. Many rituals with religious background, such as the symbolic purification of the ring with salt, are still followed today. In line with tradition, only men practice the sport professionally in Japan.

    Iaido

    Iaido is the study of swordsmanship to intuitively respond to an attack with smooth, controlled movements drawing the sword from its scabbard, striking or cutting an opponent, and then returning the sword to the scabbard. Techniques are studied from a variety of seated and standing situations.

    Ninjutsu

    Ninjutsu, sometimes used interchangeably with the modern term ninpō, is the martial art strategy and tactics of unconventional warfare, guerrilla warfare and espionage purportedly practised by the ninja.

    Kendo

    Kendo is a modern Japanese martial art, descended from kenjutsu, that uses bamboo swords as well as protective armor. Today, it is widely practiced within Japan and has spread to many other nations across the world.
